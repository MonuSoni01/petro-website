What is a Wall Plug?

Wall plugs (also known as a rawl or rawl plug, dowel or anchor) are a small, but robust hardware component that is designed to:

  • Give a secure grip to screws in hollow or brittle walls.
  • Stop screws from loosening and causing damage to the wall
  • Spread the load evenly across the surfaces like brick, concrete or drywall.

Without a wall plug, screws can slide off, causing damage or failure in the installation. This is the reason why wall plugs are essential construction equipment in commercial, residential and industrial projects.

Different Types of Wall Plugs

1. Plastic Wall Plugs

  • Material: Nylon or high-quality plastic
  • Best for: General use in brick, block or stone walls
  • Advantages:
    • Cost-effective
    • Easy to use
    • Available in a variety of sizes
  • Common Uses: Hanging shelves, mirrors, curtain rods, lighting fixtures

Petro Industech supplies premium plastic wall plugs in bulk to construction and hardware stores.

2. Nylon Wall Plugs

  • Material: Stronger nylon blend
  • Ideal for: Heavy-duty applications
  • Advantages:
    • High capacity for load capacity
    • Water and weather resistant
    • Long-lasting grip
  • Common Uses: Bathroom accessories, electrical fittings, Industrial hardware

The wall plugs made of nylon have been tested for their durability and are available in large quantities to distributors and dealers.

3. Metal Wall Plugs (Expansion Anchors)

  • Material: Steel or zinc alloy
  • Ideal for: Very massive loads on bricks or concrete
  • Advantages:
    • Fire resistant
    • Maximum strength
    • Corrosion-resistant coatings available
  • Common Applications: Structural applications, gates, railings and machinery installations

4. Self-Drilling Wall Plugs

  • Material: Plastic/nylon with sharp threads
  • Best for: Quick installation on plasterboard or drywall
  • Advantages:
    • No drilling or pre-drilling is required.
    • Reduces time and labour
    • Strong grip on hollow walls
  • Common Uses: Mounting photo frames, switches, clocks or light panels.

5. Hammer-in Plugs

  • Material: Plug made of nylon with a screw for a steel nail
  • Best For: Fast installation in masonry
  • Advantages:
    • Simple hammer-in operation
    • No screwdriver is required at first
    • Ideal for repetitive large-scale work
  • Common Uses: Secure battens, wiring channels or other lightweight structures

6. Universal Wall Plugs

  • Material: Premium nylon/plastic
  • Ideal for: Multi-surface use (brick, concrete, stone, hollow block and plasterboard)
  • Advantages:
    • Expands when solid materials are present
    • Knots made of hollow materials
    • One plug that can be used for multiple surfaces
  • Common Uses: Multi-purpose Fastener used for professional and household use

Common Mistakes to Avoid in Wall Plug Selection

  • Plugs made of plastic for heavy machinery can lead to wall damage
  • Screw size not matching the plug size - causes slippage
  • Installing plugs on walls that are damp reduces grip strength
  • The only plug type in bulk can limit the sales opportunities

Wall Plug Installation Tips (Pro Guidance)

  • Drill a hole to match the plug's size.
  • Clean up the dust before inserting the plug.
  • Make sure the screw length is 1.5x the length of the plug.
  • For loads that are heavy for heavy loads, use nylon or steel plugs.
  • For quick fixes, use self-drilling plugs.
